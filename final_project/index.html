Shredders of my Youth

The Technically-Oriented Guitar Rock of the 80s and 90s

The late 1980s and 1990s were a time of renaissance and innovation for technical guitarists. Neon green and flashy red ruled the color scheme of guitar paint jobs, and two-hand tapping, complex modal theory, and sweep arpeggios ruled the fret boards. Hair was big and musical theory knowledge (arguably) bigger. This was the time in which I started playing guitar. Guitar For the Practicing Musician magazine brought us our tablature and hidden guitar secrets in a time before the internet, and Mike Varney of Shrapnel Records brought us our heroes. The following pages are a multi-area sampling of innovative guitarists I grew up appreciating.
